Story: Learning to Park a Car

“Think about the first time you tried to park a car.

You didn’t land perfectly.

You were too left.
Then too right.
Then too forward.
Then too back.

Each time, you adjusted a little.

You didn’t teleport into the parking spot.
You slowly corrected.”

Punchline
“That slow correction is gradient descent.

AI learns like a terrible new driver.”



🎤 SPEAKER NOTES — 03_Game2_Fix_The_Model
🎤 Hook

“Okay… the AI is broken.
It thinks a pizza costs five hundred dollars.
Let’s fix it.”

🧠 Concept Explanation

“This is where learning actually happens.

The model makes a guess.
Reality gives the correct answer.

The difference between the two is the error.”

🎢 Fun Analogy

“Imagine you’re lost on a mountain at night.

You can’t see the map.
You don’t know where the village is.

All you can feel is the slope under your feet.

If the ground goes down, you step that way.
You keep stepping downhill until you can’t anymore.

That’s how the model learns.”

🤓 Fun Fact

“Large language models don’t adjust two settings like this.
They adjust trillions — all at once — without ever seeing the full picture.”

📜 Script / Key Talking Points

“The red line is the model’s guess.”

“The green points are reality.”

“That vertical distance is the error.”

“Move the weight slider. The line rotates.”

“Move the bias slider. The line shifts up or down.”

“Your goal is simple: reduce the error.”

“If you succeed, you just trained a model.”

“You manually performed backpropagation.”
